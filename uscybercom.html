U.S. Cyber Command (USCYBERCOM) Overview

USCYBERCOM

The U.S. Cyber Command is one of the combatant commands of the US department of defense. USCYBERCOM is in charge of cyber space operations and conducting offensive and defensive operations to defend both the nation and department of defense.

Key Figures

Key Figures


Cyber-Skills:

U.S. Cyber Command specifically exercises and tests their ability to conduct defensice, offensice, and information operations.

Defensive Operations:

    • Traffic monitoring
    • Identifying vulnerabilities
    • Developing security measures

Offensive Operations:

    • Network penetration
    • Operation disruption
    • Intelligence gathering


Attacks:

Disruption of Trickbot Malware (2020)

    • A cyber attack against Trickbot, a botnet used for ransomware attacks, financial gain, and corporate attacks.
    • USCYBERCOM identified and targeted Trickbot's C2 servers and infrastructure to degrade its ability to spread.
    • Identified as a threat due to its concerns for potentially disrupting the 2020 election.


Detect:

The USCYBERCOM is a government agency so direct detection would not be feasible.
Knowing USCYBERCOM's skills and past attacks can help identify their involvement in operations.

Due to their specialization in offensive operations and their recent attack towards Trickbot's infrastructure, indicators could include:

    • Network and server infrastructure penetration
    • Data poisoning to disrupt malware
    • Monitoring government media and announcements since USCYBERCOM usually partner with other companies in attacks, for example USCYBERCOM partnered with Microsoft for the Trickbot attack and Microsoft posted updates on social media regarding the attack.


Track:

The USCYBERCOM's main focus is on defending the U.S. National Security and the Department of Defense.

The best way to track USCYBERCOM's involvment in operations, North Korea would need to track major U.S. events such as elections or holidays where USCYBERCOM would be focused on protecting National Security and the DoD, where they are most likely going to conduct defensive or offensive operations. Similar to the Trickbot attack.
