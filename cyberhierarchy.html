Cyber Hierarchy

Cyber Operatives

Lazarus Group

All Tracked Names

Labyrinth Chollima, HIDDEN COBRA, Gaurdians of Peace, ZINC, NICKEL ACADEMY

Cyber-Skills

The Lazarus Group is a well know cyber operative sponsored by North Korea, which have conducted a wide array of cyber attacks. Majority of North Korean sponsored activity has been tracked to Lazarus. Below are some skills attributed to Lazarus:

  • Social engineering tactics like spear phishing
  • Remote Access Trojans
  • Ransomware deployment
  • Financial and cryptocurrency exploits

APT37

All Tracked Names

Reaper, ScarCruft, Richochet Chollima, InkySquid, Group123, TEMP.Reaper

Cyber-Skills

Active since 2012, mainly targets South Korea, Japan, Russia, Vietnam, Nepal, China, and the Middle East. Has been tracked working under Lazarus.

  • Social engineering tactics such as impersonating as others
  • Remote Access Trojans
  • Bluetooth Harvesting
  • Variety of malware

Kimsuky

All Tracked Names

STOLEN PENCIL, Thallium, Black Banshee, Velvet Chollima

Cyber-Skills

Active since 2012, Kimsuky focuses on gathering information on foreign policy and national security relating to nuclear weaponry. Its main targets are South Korea, US, Russia, Europe, and the UN

  • Social engineering tactics such as spear phishing campaigns using emails
  • Watering hole attacks
  • Malware such as CHM files

Andariel

All Tracked Names

Silent Chollima

Cyber-Skills

Active since 2009, Andariel has conducted many destructive attacks mainly targeting South Korea, military, and financial services. Andariel has also been tracked to have worked under Lazarus.

  • Ransomware attacks
  • Exploiting financial systems
  • Privilege escalation

APT38

All Tracked Names

NICKEL GLADSTONE, BeagleBoyz, Bluenoroff, Stardust Chollima

Cyber-Skills

Active since 2014, APT38 focuses on financial services including banks, casinos, and ATMs. Has been tracked to have worked under Lazarus group.

  • Social engineering tactics such as spear phishing campaigns to get users personnel information
  • Malware deployments into transaction systems
  • VPN hopping
