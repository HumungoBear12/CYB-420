Democratic People's Republic of Korea - Mission Statement

Mission Statement

Mission Statement

To exploit cyber capabilities as a key tool for asymmetric warfare, targeting adversaries’ critical infrastructure, military systems, and economic resources. The regime prioritizes cyber espionage for gathering intelligence, cybercrime for generating revenue through activities like theft, ransomware, and cryptocurrency hacking, and disruptive attacks to weaken geopolitical opponents. By using cyber operations, North Korea seeks to evade international sanctions, secure strategic advantages, and destabilize foreign governments, while maintaining deniability and reducing reliance on traditional military power.


North Koreas Objectives

- Maintain and strengthen the Kim family dynasty​

- Support the development of nuclear and missile programs.​

- Gather intelligence on adversaries and potential threats​

- Generate revenue to fund regime activities ​

- Develop asymmetric warfare capabilities

Operational and Execution Process

Training: Nurture talent in computer science domestically and internationally​

Deployment: Utilize IT workers and cyber operators globally​

Target Selection: Defense, aerospace, nuclear, engineering, and financial sectors​

Attack Execution: Malware/Ransomware deployment, Social engineering, and Vulnerability exploitation​

Data Analysis: Use stolen information to advance regime goals


Modified Mission Statement

"To employ cyber capabilities as a strategic asset for advancing regime goals, undermining adversaries, and circumventing sanctions through intelligence, technology, financial operations, espionage, cyber warfare, and information manipulation on a global scale. These efforts aim to strengthen national defense, safeguard the regime’s sovereignty, and support its long-term stability. By continuously evolving its cyber expertise, the regime seeks to enhance its global influence while countering external threats and ensuring resilience against foreign interference.".

Critical Objectives

Financial Acquisition

  • Generate revenue through cyber theft, targeting banks and cryptocurrency exchanges.
  • This funds nuclear and missile programs while bypassing sanctions.

Intelligence Gathering

  • Collect sensitive information from military and government targets.
  • This enhances strategic decision-making and supports military operations.

Technology Acquisition

  • Steal advanced technology in defense and aerospace sectors.
  • This accelerates weapons development and reduces reliance on foreign expertise.

Asymmetric Warfare Capability

  • Develop the ability to disrupt critical infrastructure of adversaries.
  • This provides a strategic deterrent without direct military confrontation.

Regime Preservation

  • Monitor and counter threats to the Kim dynasty, including tracking defectors.
  • This strengthens internal security and maintains regime control.
