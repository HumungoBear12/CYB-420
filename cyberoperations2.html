Cyber Operation: DeTankZone (North Korea)

Operation Overview

DeTankZone is a North Korean cyber espionage campaign conducted by the Lazarus group with their subgroup named BlueNoroff. This planned attack went above and beyond with their social engineering. They used source code from a game called DEFI TankLand and altered it to DeTankZone that set up their zero-day exploit of Google Chrome. People that visited the website, ended up with a script embedded in it called index.tsx to exploit CVE-2024-4947 that gave the lazarus group full control over the PCs. With full access to the PC, the group would then decide if they wannted to continue the attack based off the crypto balance of these people. It would then download a backdoor called Manuscrypt that the group has used since 2016. This is how Kapersky was able to find the Chrome vulnerability.

Offensive Cyber Strategy

Key Goals:

  • Gain cryptocurrency to further fund their cyberwarfare attacks
  • Find new exploitations

Recommended Offensive Tools/Weapons:

  • The use of malware
    • In this attack, the group used DeTankZone as a way to get their malware into the system. With the website running on the victims PCs, it attacked the V8 Javascript and WebAssembly engine zero-day as their way to ingest malware onto the system.
  • The use of social engineering
    • The Lazarus group used spear phishing emails and social media to help make this attack more believable. The use of an X account and paid LinkedIn profiles promoting the website led users to trusting the site which led to their system being breached.
  • OSINT
    • The members went into depth, targeting people that promote the use of cryptocurrency. They would comment on their X posts promoting it and make fake LinkedIn accounts from these people saying that the site was a good way to gain extra cryptocurrency in a fun way with the game. This draws in people that already have cryptocurrency wallets making it easy for them to steal from their crypt wallets.

Legal Considerations:

  • Russian Law: Federal Law on Personal data prohibits the use and access to personal data without thier consent. They also exfiltrate data outside of the country. This can be punishable up to $78,000 per indivual effected.
  • European Law: The General Data Protection Regulation focuses on personal data protection and this attack went direclty against personal data. This is punishable up to 1.4% of gloabal annual revenue or 7 million.

Defensive Cyber Strategy

Key Goals:

  • Prevent espionage and data theft.
  • Real-time monitoring and proactive threat mitigation.

Recommended Defensive Tools/Weapons:

  • Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) Solutions
    • This would be helpful against this attack because it will be monitoring over these endpoints and would be able to pick up the suspicious activity caused by their malware and backdoor.
  • Awareness training
    • Awarness training is crucial when when dealing with social engineering. If people dont know the risks of opening risky sites or downloading games online, then more situations like this happen. Especially with an attack like this, they went so in depth with the social engineering, this would be a great example of the depths people go to in order to gain access to your PC.
  • Antivirus Software (e.g., Norton)
    • This would be beneficial in a case like this because it would be able to pick the malware that had infected the host.

Use of Related Tools:

  • Social Engineering: They used spear phising emails, promoting using Twitter and paid LinkedIn. They also explained that the demo of the game was free if you downloaded it.
  • Backdoor They used Manuscrypt which is their backdoor that they have been using since 2013 and its been in countless attacks since.
