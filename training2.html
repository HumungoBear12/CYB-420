North Korean Training and Development Programs

Our Goal

The goal of this training program is to develop and implement in-house training and exercises to enhance the skills, awareness, and readiness of North Korea's cyber team. Ensuring that the team remains up to date and informed on all emerging threats, tools, and technologies, and is able to effectively respond to these challenges.

Phishing Attack Simulation

Objective: Improve North Koreas knwoledge and awareness for phishing attemots that may be targeted towards the nation of north korea. Phishing is the most common form of a social engineering attack so preventing those will cause less problems for North Korea.

Structure

The exercise will consist of three phases, each varying in time, the preparation phase, execution phase, and debriefing phase. Preparation is where both teams will plan how they will attack or defend. Execution is when the attack plan is launched and the defending responds to the attack. Debriefing is when both teams evaluate and discuss areas of improvement.

  • Preparation Phase: Creating false emails to send out to high level employees to train there awareness and knowledge.
  • Execution Phase: Send out these practice phishing attempts towards the high level employees.
  • Debriefing Phase: Reveal to the emplyess that this was an exercise and have practice videos set up to educate the employees

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Employees: Improved seduacation and awareness to prevent phishing attacks
  • Trainers: Better understanding of how phsihng tricks the user to give better practice attempts in the future

Resources Needed

  • Cyber Range: A computer or laptop
  • Tools: Mass email sender to the employees
  • Facilitators: Phishing trainers to send out real world examples to the employees

Evaluation

The High level employees are graded out if they were to determine if the email was a phishing attack or not. It will notify who clicked out the link so we know who didnt properly analyze the email.
