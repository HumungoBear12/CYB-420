National Cyber Force (NCF) Overview

National Cyber Force (NCF)

The NCF is the U.K.’s premier cyber unit, jointly operated by GCHQ, the Ministry of Defense, and MI6. It focuses on counter-terrorism, espionage, and national defense.

Key Figures

Anne Keast-Butler
Director of GCHQ
Born: 1964 - 1974, location undisclosed.
Oversees cyber defense and intelligence operations.

Emma Clarke
Lead Cyber Operations Officer at GCHQ
Born: 1982, Bradford, based in Cheltenham, U.K.
Oversees major cyber defense and offensive operations.

Key Skills & Specializations

  • Cyber Counter-Terrorism: Disrupts extremist communications and networks.
  • Espionage: Gathers intelligence on adversarial activities.
  • Defensive Operations: Protects U.K. infrastructure.
  • Offensive Cyber Warfare: Executes authorized cyber-attacks on high-risk targets.
  • Disinformation Countermeasures: Counters online disinformation and propaganda.

Key Tactics

  • Phased Takedowns: Coordinated shutdowns of propaganda websites and social media accounts.
  • DDoS Attacks: Disrupted ISIS servers to hinder communications.
  • Malware Deployment: Inserted malware to monitor and disrupt ISIS communications.
  • Account Suspensions: Worked with social media platforms for mass account and content removals.
  • AI Surveillance: Used AI to analyze communication patterns and predict ISIS’s alternative channels.

Results

  • Reduced Influence: ISIS’s online reach dropped by 60% within a month.
  • Ongoing Surveillance: Continued monitoring as ISIS shifted to other platforms.
  • Global Collaboration: Supported by international intelligence for sustained efforts against extremist content.

Methods for Detecting NCF Activities

  • Honeypot Systems: Create decoy networks to analyze NCF tactics.
  • OSINT Monitoring: Analyzes Open Source Intelligence (OSI) for NCF insights.
  • Behavioral Analytics: Uses machine learning to detect traffic patterns consistent with NCF campaigns.

Operation Blackout (2022)

  • Target: ISIS’s main communication networks, including encrypted channels, social media, and propaganda sites.
  • Objective: Disrupt ISIS’s digital reach to limit recruitment and communication, enhancing security for NATO and allied nations.
  • Motivation: Reduce radicalization, prevent recruitment, and weaken ISIS's operational capabilities.
