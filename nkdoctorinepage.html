China's Cyber Laws

Data Security Law

China's Data security law has a goal of regulating data processing, ensuring data security, promoting development and utilization of data, protecting the lawful rights and interests of individuals and organizations, and safeguarding the sovereignty, security, and development interests of the state.

This law is a response the U.S. CLOUD Act, which grants the U.S. government to obtain data from U.S. companies storing data outside of the U.S.

The data security law requires data localization for sensitive data and has strict regulation on cross-border data transfers.It allows for the Chinese government to dictate data practices for companies.

Personal Information Protection Law

China's Personal Information Protection Law has a goal of limiting the processing and storing of personal information.

It puts an emphasis on requiring consent from individuals before companies collect or process their personal information.It also limits the amount of data can held and to store it, it must be necessary for a clearly defined purpose.

This law is modeled after the EU's general data protection regulation. Simalarly, they both define personal data very broadly, leaving room for the governments to decide whats included case to case.

Development of IT Systems

Emphasizes the development of IT systems for the state, with mandatory state control over technology infrastructure.

Imprisonment

Punitive measures, including imprisonment, for cybercrimes or violations of state cyber policies.

Public Confession

Cybercriminals or offenders may be forced to publicly confess their crimes.

Death

In extreme cases, cybercrimes can result in capital punishment, depending on the severity and impact on the state.
