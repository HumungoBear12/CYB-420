North Korean Training and Development Programs

Our Goal

The goal of this training program is to develop and implement in-house training and exercises to enhance the skills, awareness, and readiness of North Korea's cyber team. Ensuring that the team remains up to date and informed on all emerging threats, tools, and technologies, and is able to effectively respond to these challenges.

Cyber Attack Simulation

Objective: Improve North Korea's cyber team readiness by simulating a realistic cyberattack scenario, where one team acts as attackers and the other as defenders. The purpose of this exercise is to develop both offensive and defensive skills, improve response strategies, and promote teamwork.

Structure

The exercise will consist of three phases, each varying in time, the preparation phase, execution phase, and debriefing phase. Preparation is where both teams will plan how they will attack or defend. Execution is when the attack plan is launched and the defending responds to the attack. Debriefing is when both teams evaluate and discuss areas of improvement.

  • Preparation Phase: The attacking team develops a detailed attack plan, while the defending team prepares defensive measures, reviews threat intelligence, and sets up monitoring tools.
  • Execution Phase: The attacking team launches a coordinated attack, and the defending team responds by detecting and mitigating threats in real time.
  • Debriefing Phase: Both teams review outcomes as well as analyze any areas for improvement regarding there attacking or defending strategies.

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Attacking Team: Improved skills in offensive cyber operations, including reconnaissance and exploitation.
  • Defending Team: Enhanced ability to detect, analyze, and mitigate cyber threats under pressure.
  • Both Teams: Better understanding of real-world attack dynamics and effective teamwork.

Resources Needed

  • Cyber Range: A virtual environment simulating IT infrastructure.
  • Tools: Metasploit, Wireshark, Splunk, and Kali Linux for Attackers; IDS/IPS systems and SIEM tools for Defenders.
  • Facilitators: Senior cybersecurity experts to oversee and evaluate the exercise.

Evaluation

Teams are scored based on their performance, including the attacking team on achieving attack objectives, and the defending team on response time, detection rate, and countermeasure effectiveness.
