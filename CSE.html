Communications Security Establishment (CSE) Overview

Communications Security Establishment (CSE)

The Communications Security Establishment (CSE) is Canada's national cryptologic agency, responsible for foreign signals intelligence and cybersecurity. It protects government networks, provides cybersecurity advice, and conducts offensive and defensive cyber operations to safeguard Canadian interests. Additionally, CSE plays a crucial role in gathering intelligence to inform government decision-making and protect national security.

Operation to Disrupt China State-Sponsered Botnet

The Volt Typhoon attack was a cyber threat launched by a Chinese state-sponsored hacking group, known as "Flax Typhoon" or "Integrity Technology Group." This group compromised hundreds of thousands of consumer devices, such as routers, IP cameras, and DVRs, turning them into a botnet that could be used for various malicious activities, including data theft and disruption of critical infrastructure. The main motivation was aimed at potential espionage and disruption of critical infrastructure in the states. The CSE aided the states in defending this attack by disrupting the KV botnet.
